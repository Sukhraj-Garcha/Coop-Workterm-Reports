About

For 40 years, Camis Inc. has been providing reservations and park management solutions for government operated parks, marinas and harbours. The founder of the company, Doug Hall, saw the need for an improved reservation system at a campground that he often visited and decided to provide the solution himself. In 1980, Camis (then known as Bluewater Parklands Management) began operation with just 8 seasonal employees and Doug Hall. 7 Years later, in 1987, Bluewater Parklands Management was officially registered as a company under the name Camis and secured contracts with Craigleith, Point Farms & Sauble Falls, Ontario. Throughout the past four decades, the Camis product has undergone many adaptations, from phone calls to lists on paper to the feature-rich software that is available today. Currently, Camis has 297 employees and has partnered with over 450 government operated facilities in the United States and Canada. With new features being brainstormed and introduced frequently, it is clear that the Camis software will continue to grow and provide convenience to make camping a better experience for both campers and campground workers.

Job Description

As a member of the Quality Assurance team in Product Delivery, the majority of my tasks revolved around ensuring that the Camis software worked as desired and would be able to provide its users with a simple user experience that fulfilled its purpose. I was directly responsible for checking all aspects of the software to ensure that no bugs were present in a UAT environment before it was deployed to production.

The interesting part about this term is that the work that I was working on was not stuff that I have really learned about (at least thus far) in my education. My day-to-day responsibilities involved completing manual regression testing for specific releases, manually verifying tickets that have been fixed, verifying UAT feedback tickets provided by clients, creating bug tickets to report issues found during testing, and updating existing test cycles to account for new/updated features. All of these are topics that are not really discussed much in class, so it gave me the opportunity to learn new things almost on a weekly basis since I was exposed to a lot of different tasks and methods of testing.

These past 4 months provided a great challenge because I pretty much had to learn new things on the go. The majority of my work was done using the Jira platform, a platform that I had zero experience with and barely even knew what it was about prior to this term. Needless to say, I was definitely slightly overwhelmed with all of the different options/functions that Jira had at first. However, after 4 months of using the platform everyday, I can safely say that I am an expert. This work term also gave me the challenging opportunity of working in a fast-paced environment with strict deadlines. The new releases for 3 or 4 clients at a time had to be completely tested, with all concerning issues reported only in a few days so that they could be deployed to production on time. This type of environment gave me the opportunity to greatly improve my time-management skills. Although the combination of working in such a fast-paced environment for the first time and having to learn new technologies and skills was scary at first, by the end of the term, I was able to overcome it all and come out as a much stronger individual.

  • PROFESSIONAL & ETHICAL BEHAVIOUR - Teamwork
    Another one of my goals for my placement at Camis was to gain a strong understanding of what it means to work in an Agile Work Environment and improve my ability to work in a team in an actual work setting. A large majority of modern software companies follow the pillars of an Agile Work Environment. Learning about what this means and how to be effective in such an environment will greatly improve my ability to integrate with the team and be a productive member of the organization in future employment opportunities. After working with an Agile team for 4 months, I can say with confidence that I have a much stronger understanding of what it means to work in an Agile Work Environment. This work term has allowed me to actually experience what it means to work in a large Agile team (instead of working in a small team for a group project in school). Camis has many different squads who are all in charge of a very specific aspect of the Camis software (sales squad, booking squad, QA, etc.). Throughout my time at Camis, I have seen how all of these squads work together and communicate through Slack/meetings to complete their goals for the current sprint. I have had to pose questions to people from almost every squad in order to achieve my goals, thus giving me a strong understanding of how an Agile team, despite having smaller teams that focus on a particular thing, are able to work and communicate together to deliver an end product.
  • PERSONAL LEARNING GOAL
    A personal learning goal that I wanted to achieve for myself was to develop a closer bond with my teammates, beyond just simply being co-workers. This goal will not necessarily help my career beyond expanding my network, but this is just something that I wanted to accomplish over the course of the term just for me. After my time with the QA team, I can say with certainty that my bond with some of my team members has gone beyond that of co-workers. Everyday, I join stand-up a few minutes early to have personal conversations with my team members. I have found that this not only improves our friendship, but that it is also a nice break (especially in a virtual environment) from work related tasks. Additionally, I frequently engage in non-work related conversations in Slack and have also started to play video games outside of work hours with some of my co-workers.

Conclusion

Overall, I could not have asked for a better first work term experience. Camis is a wonderful company and gave me the chance to meet and work with amazing people! I was able to complete challenging (but rewarding) work and gained many valuable skills that will help my employability in the future. Quality Assurance is not typically touched on in class, and before this term, this field of computer science was something that I did not think much about. However, the last 4 months have taught me just how important the QA role is in running a successful company.
